PRIVACY POLICY AND SECURITY NOTICE

By: Naval Special Warfare
Posted: March 4, 2020


This official Leap Frogs - U.S. Navy Parachute Team website (NAVYLEAPFROGS.COM) is a United States Government website provided as a public service by Commander, Naval Special Warfare Command. Information presented on NAVYLEAPFROGS.COM is public information. Unless otherwise indicated, the information presented on NAVYLEAPFROGS.COM may be distributed or copied. Use of appropriate byline/photo/image credits is requested, i.e., “U.S. Navy”.

Unauthorized attempts to upload or change information on this website is strictly prohibited and may be punishable under the Computer Fraud and Abuse Act of 1986 and the National Information Infrastructure Protection Act.

For website security purposes and to ensure that this service remains available to all users, this government contracted computer system employs software programs to monitor network traffic to identify unauthorized attempts to upload or change information, or otherwise cause damage. Except for authorized law enforcement investigations, no other attempts are made to identify individual users or their usage habits. Raw data logs are used for no other purposes and are scheduled for regular destruction in accordance with National Archives and Records Administration General Schedule 20.

For website navigation purposes only, cookies are used in a limited manner. The use of cookies is an industry-standard practice, and many major websites use them to provide useful features for their customers. Most browsers are initially set up to accept cookies. If you'd prefer, you can set yours to refuse cookies. However, you may not be able to take full advantage of this website if you do.

Cookies are pieces of information that a website transfers to your desktop or mobile device for record-keeping purposes. Cookies can make the web more useful by storing information about your preferences on a particular site. Cookies don't collect or store Personally Identifiable Information (PII) such as name, address, phone or email. They do however, identify a user's desktop or mobile device.

DISCLOSURE

Providing information is strictly voluntary. The Naval Special Warfare Command does not share information with any person or other federal agency unless pursuant to exceptions listed in the Privacy Act. All information collected is stored on secure dedicated servers. Access to the database containing individual records is controlled and restricted by the Naval Special Warfare Command.

TYPES OF INFORMATION WE COLLECT

Location information, including location information provided by a desktop or mobile device or any other device interacting with the website, or associated with an Internet Protocol (IP) address.

HOW WE COLLECT YOUR INFORMATION

We collect information using analytics tools, including third-party website analytic tools. We use the analytics tools to collect and analyze statistical data about your activity on NAVYLEAPFROGS.COM. This data is used to improve your user experience on NAVYLEAPFROGS.COM, understand web traffic patterns, identify opportunities to improve the overall quality of the site, and for advertising and marketing.

AUTHORITY

SHARING YOUR INFORMATION WITH OTHERS

Federal law states that the United States Navy will not release any information offered by you to any person or organization outside the Department of Defense.

Privacy choices you make on third-party platform's don't apply on NAVYLEAPFROGS.COM. The Department of the Navy is not responsible for the privacy practices on a third-party website or platform.

ACCESSABILITY

The United States Navy is dedicated to making all information and technology available to individuals with disabilities. In 1998, Section 508 of the Rehabilitation Act Amendments, as amended by the Workforce Investment Act of 1998, issued accessibility standards setting forth electronic and information technology performance criteria.
